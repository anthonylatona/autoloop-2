# Email Optimization Goals

## What This Document Is
A cold outreach HTML email. It will be evaluated as if it were about to be sent
to a list of B2B prospects. It needs to land in the inbox, get opened, and
generate a single response or booked meeting.

The audience is a skeptical, busy decision-maker who receives dozens of cold
emails per week. They delete anything that feels like a mass blast.

## What We Are Optimizing For
Score the email on these five dimensions (100 points total):

1. **Deliverability** (25%) — Will spam filters pass this? Score as follows:
   - Spam-trigger words (10 points) — Count instances of: free, guaranteed, urgent, act now, limited time, risk-free, winner, revolutionary. Deduct 2 points per instance.
   - Format issues (8 points) — Deduct for: excessive punctuation (!!! or ???), ALL CAPS phrases, image-heavy structure with little text.
   - Compliance (7 points) — Must have unsubscribe link (4 points) and physical address (3 points).

2. **Prospect-Fit Signals** (25%) — Does this feel written for a specific person, 
   or is it obviously a mass blast? Score as follows:
   - Template structure readiness (5 points) — Binary: Does the email contain placeholder fields (e.g., "[PROSPECT COMPANY]") or conditional logic (e.g., "I noticed...recently") that *enable* personalization? Yes = 5 points. No = 0 points. This is table stakes; do not award partial credit.
   - Trigger specificity & verification (20 points) — The email must cite a *specific, verifiable trigger* (e.g., "hired 2–3 new sales reps") AND include one concrete data point that anchors why that trigger matters (e.g., "new hires typically create 30% more pipeline churn"). Deduct 10 points if trigger is present but unverified or vague; deduct 20 points if no trigger or only generic language ("busy decision-makers," "growing teams").

3. **Clarity & Conciseness** (20%) — Is the value proposition clear in the first
   two sentences? Is there one obvious ask, or are there multiple competing CTAs?
   Does the email respect the reader's time, or does it ramble? Could it be 40%
   shorter without losing meaning?

4. **Proof & Credibility** (22%) — Are claims backed by specifics? "300% ROI" with no
   source is worse than no stat at all. Score as follows:
   - Specific evidence (12 points) — Stats must include context (e.g., "1,200+ customers" is good; "saves time" is vague). Case studies, benchmarks, or third-party validation count. Deduct 2 points per unsupported claim.
   - Low-friction ask (10 points) — Is the ask a 15-min demo or a full product consultation? A quick call beats a long commitment. Deduct 3 points if ask feels heavy.

5. **Sender Credibility** (8%) — Is the sender a real human or a faceless brand?
   Does the signature include name and email? Is there a reason to trust this person?
   Deduct 2 points if signature lacks name or email; deduct 4 points if sender appears
   to be a bot or generic alias. Phone number is a plus but not required.
